What is a Scholarship?

A scholarship is a financial award typically awarded to students for academic merit. This is a great way to pay for college, as you do not have to pay any of the money back. There are many common misconceptions about how to qualify for, apply for, and receive scholarships, but the information below will clear up any uncertainty.



Who can apply for a scholarship?

Any student can apply for a scholarship. The main concern is which scholarships you are able to apply for. Many scholarships have various conditions and prerequisites that may cater towards certain students. Some common requirements often include gender, ethnicity, income, citizenship, intended major, and location. Many scholarships also provide characteristics that will provide a students with the best possible chances of receiving the scholarship. Many of these factors include GPA, course rigour, extracurriculars, individual grades, leadership, and voulenteer work.



What if I have bad grades?

While grades do play a vital factor in your qualification for scholarships, they are not the only factor that matters. Rather than hurting your chances with low grades, it is best to maintain your GPA to be no lower than a 2.5.



What if my family has a high income?

Many scholarships do cater to students coming from families with lower incomes, but there are still many scholarships out there that do not take financial need into account. Discovering these scholarships is just a matter of researching.





What scholarships are availible for me?

Some of the most notable scholarships in and out of Texas include:



The Terry Foundation: The Terry Traditional Scholarship

The Terry Traditional scholarship is a program that allows for low income students who have shown academic merit to have a fully paid college experience at one of many Texas universities.



Coca-Cola Scholars Foundation: Coke Scholars

A merit-based scholarship program offered to seniors in high school who have a strong desire to help others and are committed to making a significant impact on their schools and communities. This $20,000 prize is awarded to 150 Coca-Cola Scholars each year.



Jack Kent Cooke Foundation: Cooke College Scholars

A national scholarship provider dedicated to advancing the education of high achieving high school seniors who seek to attend and graduate from some of the nation's best four-year colleges and universities. Students must provide documentation of financial need.



The George and Mary Josephine Hamman Foundation: The George and Mary Josephine Hamman Foundation Scholarship

This Texas-based foundation has been in operation for 66 years helping students pursue higher education through scholarships, promoting the arts, funding scientific projects, assisting churches, associations, and conventions of churches in the advancement of religion, helping colleges and universities with operating and capital needs, helping the less fortunate, and supporting hospitals, medical schools, and research facilities. The Foundation has provided $108.9 million in grants and scholarships. The scholarship is open to applicants who are US citizens residing in the counties of Brazoria, Chambers, Fort Bend, Galveston, Harris, Liberty, Montgomery, or Waller. They must also have an ACT score of 21 or a SAT score of 1000 and show financial need.

Amazon Future Engineer: Amazon Future Engineer Scholarship

The Amazon Future Engineer scholarship program assists high school seniors who have completed an advanced placement computer science course and plan to continue their education at an accredited four-year college or university majoring in computer science, software engineering, computer engineering or other computer related field of study.
