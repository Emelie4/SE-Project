What is a Scholarship?

A scholarship is a financial award typically awarded to students for academic merit. This is a great way to pay for college, as you do not have to pay any of the money back. There are many common misconceptions about how to qualify for, apply for, and receive scholarships, but the information below will clear up any uncertainty.



Who can apply for a scholarship?

Any student can apply for a scholarship. The main concern is which scholarships you can apply for. Many scholarships have various conditions and prerequisites that may cater to certain students. Some common requirements often include gender, ethnicity, income, citizenship, intended major, and location. Many scholarships also provide characteristics that will provide students with the best possible chances of receiving the scholarship. Many of these factors include GPA, course rigor, extracurriculars, individual grades, leadership, and volunteer work.



What if I have bad grades?

While grades do play a vital factor in your qualification for scholarships, they are not the only factor that matters. Rather than hurting your chances with low grades, it is best to maintain your GPA to no lower than 2.5.



What if my family has a high income?

Many scholarships do cater to students coming from families with lower incomes, but there are still many scholarships out there that do not take financial need into account. Discovering these scholarships is just a matter of researching.





What scholarships are availible for me?

Some of the most notable scholarships in and out of Texas include:



The Terry Foundation: The Terry Traditional Scholarship

The Terry Traditional scholarship is a program that allows low-income students who have shown academic merit to have a fully paid college experience at one of many Texas universities.



Coca-Cola Scholars Foundation: Coke Scholars

A merit-based scholarship program offered to seniors in high school who have a strong desire to help others and are committed to making a significant impact on their schools and communities. This $20,000 prize is awarded to 150 Coca-Cola Scholars each year.



Jack Kent Cooke Foundation: Cooke College Scholars

A national scholarship provider dedicated to advancing the education of high achieving high school seniors who seek to attend and graduate from some of the nation's best four-year colleges and universities. Students must provide documentation of financial need.



The George and Mary Josephine Hamman Foundation: The George and Mary Josephine Hamman Foundation Scholarship

This Texas-based foundation has been in operation for 66 years helping students pursue higher education through scholarships, promoting the arts, funding scientific projects, assisting churches, associations, and conventions of churches in the advancement of religion, helping colleges and universities with operating and capital needs, helping the less fortunate, and supporting hospitals, medical schools, and research facilities. The Foundation has provided $108.9 million in grants and scholarships. The scholarship is open to applicants who are US citizens residing in the counties of Brazoria, Chambers, Fort Bend, Galveston, Harris, Liberty, Montgomery, or Waller. They must also have an ACT score of 21 or a SAT score of 1000 and show financial need.



The Herbert Lehman Education Fundation: Herbert Lehman Scholarship

A national, highly competitive annual scholarship given to qualified, exceptional graduating high school seniors and undergraduate students (Not available for juniors and beyond). In honor of the former governor and senator from New York, this scholarship was established in 1964, recognising Lehman’s integrity, bravery, and commitment to numerous civil rights causes. Applicants must demonstrate financial need through FASFA, a record of academic success, and a clear dedication to public service.



Amazon Future Engineer: Amazon Future Engineer Scholarship

The Amazon Future Engineer scholarship program assists high school seniors who have completed an advanced placement computer science course and plan to continue their education at an accredited four-year college or university majoring in computer science, software engineering, computer engineering or other computer related field of study. Winners of the award receive $10,000 at an accredited four-year college or university as well as a paid summer internship at Amazon after their freshman year of college.



Jackie Robinson Foundation: JRF Scholars

JRF Scholars receive college scholarships of up to $35,000 over four years and are financially sponsored to attend JRF‘s annual, four-day “Mentoring and Leadership Conference“ in New York City and other events throughout the year. Aside from generous financial support, Scholars benefit from a comprehensive, four-year program that includes career guidance, internship and permanent job placement, travel abroad, and leadership and practical life skills training.



Ron Brown Scholar Program: Ron Brown Scholarship

The Ron Brown Scholarship honors the legacy of Ronald H. Brown through a selective scholarship program that advances higher education for community-minded and intellectually gifted African Americans. RBSP competitively awards 45 – 50 four-year $40,000 scholarships ($10,000 each year) to the most talented and economically-challenged high school seniors who demonstrate a keen interest in public service, community engagement, business entrepreneurship and global citizenship – the four pillars of the late Ronald H. Brown’s mission to uplift people of all races, cultures and ethnicities – especially our disadvantaged youth. Recipients may use the renewable scholarships to attend an accredited four-year college or university of their choice within the United States. Ron Brown scholarships are not limited to any specific field or career objective and may be used to pursue any academic discipline. Since the Program’s inception in 1996, more than 450 students have been designated as Ron Brown Scholars. Applicants for the Ron Brown Scholarship MUST be Black/African American, US citizens or permanent residents, and a current high school senior at the time of their application.



Lockheed Martin: Lockheed Martin Scholarship

Welcome to the Lockheed Martin STEM Scholarship Program! Whether you are a student, parent, family member, instructor, school administrator, or just interested in STEM education, we are excited to share more about our efforts to support the academic endeavors of college-level learners across the nation. Lockheed Martin launched the STEM Scholarship Program in 2018 as part of investment in the next generation of STEM talent. We are committed to providing access to pathways through education and into the STEM workforce for all students, particularly those from diverse backgrounds and communities historically underrepresented in STEM fields. Each year, the STEM Scholarship Program awards $10,000 to 200 recipients studying engineering or computer science at a 4-year college or university. Recipients may renew the scholarship up to three times for a total potential value of $40,000 per student. Applications for the new cycle will open in January 2023.



Future Google: Generation Google Scholarship (North America)

The Generation Google Scholarship was established to help aspiring students pursuing computer science degrees excel in technology and become leaders in the field. Selected students will receive $10,000 USD (for those studying in the US) or $5,000 CAD (for those studying in Canada) for the 2022-2023 school year. The Generation Google Scholarship will be awarded based on the strength of each candidate's commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion, demonstrated leadership, and academic performance. The program is open to students who meet all the minimum qualifications and we strongly encourage students that identify with groups historically excluded from the technology industry, including Women, Black/African American, Hispanic/Latinx, American Indian, Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander, to apply. Applicants are not required to currently be enrolled in an Associates, Bachelors, Masters, or PhD program for the 2021-2022 academic year to apply. Recipients of one of the following Google Scholarships: Generation Google Scholarship, Generation Google Scholarship: for women in gaming, Generation Google Scholarship: for supply chain and fulfillment, Google Lime Scholarship, Google Student Veterans of America Scholarship, Women Techmakers Scholarship for Computer Science, and Women Techmakers Scholarship for Gaming are not eligible to reapply.
